Code Of Conduct

Introduction

DSC ATC is dedicated to providing an inclusive and harassment-free experience for participants at all of our events. We are working events intended for networking and collaboration in the community. They exist to encourage the open exchange of ideas and expression and require an environment that recognizes the inherent worth of every person and group. While at DSC ATC or related social events, any participants, including members, speakers, attendees, volunteers, sponsors, exhibitors, booth staff and anyone else, should not engage in harassment in any form.

Photography is encouraged, but other participants must be given a reasonable chance to opt out from being photographed. If they object to the taking of their photograph, comply with their request. It is inappropriate to take photographs in contexts where people have a reasonable expectation of privacy (in bathrooms or where participants are sleeping).

Participants asked to stop any harassing behavior are expected to comply immediately. As this is an event, we like to explicitly note that the hacks created at our hackathon are equally subject to the anti-harassment policy.

In summary, be nice to each other and help others in need

Expected behaviour

All event participants are expected to behave in accordance with professional standards, with both this Code of Conduct as well as their respective college policies governing appropriate workplace behavior, and applicable laws.

Reporting

If you feel uncomfortable or think there may be a potential violation of the code of conduct, please report it to any volunteer present either personally or at the event or contact us at dscatc@gmail.com. All reporters have the right to remain anonymous.
